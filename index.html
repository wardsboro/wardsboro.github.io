Parade Route

The parade travels along Main Street and back again in Wardsboro, Vermont. The Street Fair is along Main Street on both sides. Listen for the ringing of the Church Bell that signals the start of the parade at 10 a.m. Don't forget ... the parade makes two trips ... both up and down Main Street. Ribbons to the parade floats and participants are awarded on the return trip so stay to see who this year's winners will be.


Our Story

The Street Fair & Parade evolved from a need to raise funds for the Methodist Church which is now the Yoked Parish. A few farsighted souls came up with the idea of a simple street fair with a novelty booth, handicraft booth, games for kids, homemade sandwiches, pie slices, and a little parade with a few floats and kids on bikes. Not only were funds left over for the Church, but the community began to be involved because it was so much fun. The funds are now spread throughout the community as well as the church for those in need.

Volunteering And Donating

Helping out at the 4th of July Street Fair & Parade has become a tradition in Wardsboro. It is part of the fun of offering entertainment and good food to several thousand visitors. To volunteer before, during or after the event, send an email to wardsboroparade (at) gmail.com
